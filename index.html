History

The word Ayurveda derived from AYU and VEDA. AYU means life VEDA means science or knowledge. Ayurveda means the science of life. Charaka defines "That science is designated as Ayurveda which deals with advantage and disadvantage as well as happy and unhappy states of life along with what is good and bad for life, its measurement and the life itself .Ayurveda is not only a system of medicine but also a way of life for complete positive health and spiritual attainments.Ayurveda believes that positive health is the basis for attaining four cherished goals of life .

Modern medicine is based on physics, Chemistry, Botany, and Zoology. Ayurveda is based on Indian Philosophy.The book on diseases of horses is called Shalihotra Samhita which contains 12000 verses, which is equivalent to the number of verses contained in Charaka Samhita. The similarity does not end with the number of verses. Just as Ayurveda is divided into 8 sections, this science has also been divided into 8 sections.Buddhism has contributed a great deal to the development of Ayurveda. A new branch of medicine based on mercury was innovated by Nagarjuna while practicing alchemy.
